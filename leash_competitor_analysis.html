Agentic Payments: the field, and where Leash actually wins

A scan of the "agents that can pay" market as of mid-2026 — who's building it, how they differ, and the one defensible wedge left for a non-custodial, multi-chain control tower.

Prepared 2026-06-21· Scope: agent-payment infra, wallets, protocols, governance· Model: non-custodial prepaid-credit SaaS
TL;DR

The rails are commoditizing (x402, AP2, Visa/Mastercard schemes are free and standardizing) and wallet-with-spend-limits is already crowded (Coinbase, Crossmint, Nevermined). You will not out-infra a16z- and Coinbase-funded incumbents. The open lane is the human-facing control + governance layer: a neutral, non-custodial, chain- and model-agnostic "control tower" aimed at operators, not crypto devs — riding the rails and using their wallets as plumbing, not rebuilding them.

1 The landscape splits into four layers

Almost everything in "AI agents that pay" falls into one of these. Knowing which layer you're in tells you who you compete with — and who you sit on top of.

Layer A · Protocols

Payment standards

x402 (Coinbase), AP2 (Google), OpenAI ACP, Visa Trusted Agent, Mastercard Agent Pay. Free, open, converging. These are tailwinds, not rivals — Leash speaks them.

Layer B · Wallet / signing infra

Give an agent a wallet

Coinbase AgentKit + Agentic Wallets, Crossmint, Privy, Turnkey, Openfort. SDKs with spend caps + allowlists. Crowded & well-funded — use as plumbing.

Layer C · Custodial / regulated FIs

Hold funds, move money

Catena Labs (regulated AI-native bank, $48M), Skyfire (agent payment network). Heavy compliance moat. A different, capital-intensive game.

Layer D · Monetization & control

Billing, guardrails, oversight

Nevermined (billing + hard limits), Payman (AI pays humans). This is Leash's neighborhood — and the least saturated for an app, not an API.

3 Where Leash is — and isn't — unique

✗ Not unique (don't claim these)

  • Spending limits + allowlists. Table stakes — Coinbase, Crossmint, Nevermined all ship them.
  • Human-approval-over-threshold. Crossmint lists this explicitly.
  • Audit log of payments. Standard across the field.
  • An agent wallet / signing. Privy, Turnkey, Coinbase own this; don't rebuild it.
  • The XRPL payment loop itself. x402 is now an open standard with giant backers.

✓ Defensible wedge (lean in)

  • An app, not an SDK. Rivals sell APIs to developers. Leash is a control tower a non-technical operator opens and watches.
  • Neutral across chains AND model providers. Coinbase is Base-centric; most tie you to their stack. Leash = Switzerland (XRPL + Solana + L2s, any model via a gateway).
  • Governance depth as the product — the policy engine, approve/deny, kill switch as the core UX, not a config flag.
  • Non-custodial by design — sidesteps the money-transmitter burden Catena/Skyfire take on.
  • Prepaid-credit simplicity for the AI — buy credits, pick a model, go; no BYOK friction for normal users.
One-line positioning

"The neutral control tower for agents that spend — non-custodial, any chain, any model, run by a human who stays in charge." You sit above x402/AP2 and on top of Crossmint/Coinbase/Turnkey wallets, owning the layer they treat as an afterthought: oversight, governance, and operator UX.

4 Three approaches to get there

A · Control plane on top of the rails RECOMMENDED

Don't build wallet infra or rails. Integrate x402/AP2 for settlement and use Crossmint / Coinbase / Turnkey for non-custodial wallets. Leash owns the policy engine + human oversight + multi-chain/multi-model dashboard + prepaid AI credits.

+ Fastest to ship · rides the tailwinds · capital-light · keeps your moat (UX/governance) — your own CLAUDE.md already bets here.
− You depend on partners' wallets; differentiation must stay in the control layer.

B · Vertical SaaS (own one niche end-to-end)

Pick a single high-value use case — e.g. autonomous research/data buying or SMB procurement agents — and own the whole non-custodial flow there with deep, domain-specific policy.

+ Clear buyer, sharper story, less head-on with horizontal infra.
− Smaller TAM per vertical; you must actually nail one workflow.

C · Open-source the governance layer

Make the policy engine the standard guardrail others embed (open-source core, hosted control tower for revenue). Credibility + grants + ecosystem gravity.

+ Distribution & defensibility via adoption; great for XRPL Grants narrative.
− Monetization is slower/indirect; needs community momentum.

Recommended sequence: A now (ship the control tower on borrowed rails + wallets), seed C in parallel (open-source the policy engine for credibility/grants), keep B as the go-to-market wedge (lead with one vertical even while the platform is horizontal).

5 Strategic risks to price in

  • Platform risk. Coinbase/Google could fold "oversight UX" into their free SDKs. Counter: stay neutral (multi-chain + multi-provider) — that's the one thing a Coinbase or Google structurally won't be.
  • Commoditization. If guardrails become a checkbox everywhere, "depth of governance" must be visibly better (richer policy DSL, real-time approvals, team roles, spend analytics).
  • Custody creep. The moment prepaid credits fund third-party merchants, you re-enter money-transmitter territory. Keep AI credits closed-loop; keep merchant payment non-custodial. decided
  • Distribution. Infra players sell to devs who already build agents. An operator-facing app needs its own demand-gen — the vertical wedge (B) is how you get a first audience.
